1.4 billion population...                         9.7 million square kilometers... 5000 years of history...      

"Which country will come into your mind?"
"China."
China has long been known for its large population.

In recent years, China has developed rapidly, both
in terms of economy and global influence. In this process, China’s huge population has made a significant contribution.

A huge population base means a huge labor force, which provides necessary assistance and a solid foundation for development.
"However,"

The huge population base is like a double-edged sword, the demographic dividend also
poses challenges to China’s society and development. One of the most critical issues is:

uneven population distribution.
Transportation.
Water resource.
Many factors affect the distribution of population.
Due to the influence of terrain and natural environment,
the distribution of roads is mainly concentrated in the
western part of the province.

The population distribution of Taiwan Province is also
concentrated in a few cities in the western part of the island.

We have reason to believe that there is a certain internal
connection between these geometries.

Analyzing and understanding these relationships will
help solve the problem of uneven population distribution
in China.

Week3：Data Processing and Analysis
Spot the pattern.
Step1: Find the intersections of road and railroad

Based on the collected data, in order to obtain information that reflects the transportation and can be used for further analysis, I intersected the shapefiles of road and railroad, then extracted the intersection points as a new layer.
Step2: Buffer the intersection points

Given that the impact of traffic on the surrounding area is diffuse, I used buffer to reflect its influence.
Step3: Find the intersections of transportation and river

By intersecting the shapefile of the river distribution with the buffer area generated in the previous step, I got points where traffic and water resources are relatively abundant.
Interestingly, there are more such points in densely populated areas.

The color of the basemap varies from yellow to red, representing the increasing population density. As you can see, there are significantly more points around the red area than the lighter color area. To some extent, this reflects the relationship between population distribution and the aforementioned two factors.
Relationship between elevation and points of interest is impressive.

The color of the basemap from green to red shows the altitude from low to high. The points of interest are mainly concentrated in areas with low altitude and relatively flat terrain. Although altitude does not seem to have a direct impact on the distribution of population, it indirectly affects the distribution of population by influencing the distribution of water resources and transportation.
Same thing happened on land cover

The color of the basemap from blue to red shows the land cover from high to low. It is worth noting that the population and points of interest are tend to concentrated in areas with lower land cover, the causality among them is difficult to judge. It also reflects that the impact of human activities on the natural environment cannot be ignored.
This figure shows the model for the previous steps.
Step4: Calculate the points of interest in the provinces

The color of the basemap from light to dark reflects the population of China’s provinces from small to large. I used summarize within to calculate how many points of interest are in each province.
Further processing of data

Considering that the area of each province is different, I divided the number of points of interest obtained in each province by the area of the province. It can be seen that the value obtained has a close relationship with the population of each province.
